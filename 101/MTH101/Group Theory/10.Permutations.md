$Perm(S)$ is the group under the binary operation of composition
For any positive integer $n$ , we define $S_{n}$ to be the group of permutations of $\{1, 2, \dots n \}$

### Array Notation

Let $\sigma \in S_{n}$ . Then, we can write $\sigma$ as a $2$x$n$ array as follows:

$$
\begin{bmatrix}
1 & 2 & 3 & \dots & n  \\
\sigma(1) & \sigma(2) & \sigma(3) & \dots & \sigma(n)
\end{bmatrix}
$$

***Example:***
$n=5$ , $\sigma(1)=2 , \sigma(2) = 5 , \sigma(3) = 4, \sigma(4) = 3 , \sigma(5) = 1$
$$
\begin{bmatrix}
1 & 2 & 3 & 4 & 5 \\
2 & 5 & 4 & 3 & 1
\end{bmatrix}
$$

***Example:***
$$
\begin{align}
\sigma ^{-1} &=
\begin{bmatrix}
1  & 2 & 3  & 4  & 5 \\
5  &  1  & 4  &  3  & 2
\end{bmatrix} \\ \\
\tau &= 
\begin{bmatrix}
1  & 2  & 3  & 4  &  5 \\
3  & 2  & 1  & 5  & 4
\end{bmatrix} \\ \\
\sigma \circ \tau &=
\begin{bmatrix}
1  & 2  & 3  & 4  & 5 \\
2  & 5  & 4  & 3  & 1
\end{bmatrix}
\cdot
\begin{bmatrix}
1  & 2  & 3  & 4  & 5  \\
3  & 2  & 1  & 5  & 4
\end{bmatrix} \\ \\
&=
\begin{bmatrix}
1  & 2  & 3  & 4  & 5 \\
4  & 5  & 2  & 1  & 3
\end{bmatrix}
\end{align}
$$
