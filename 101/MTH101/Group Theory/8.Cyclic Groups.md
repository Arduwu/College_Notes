#### Theorem
Let $G$ be a group. Let $S$ be a set of subgroups of $G$. Then, the intersection of all subgroups in $S$ is a subgroup of $G$

***Proof:***
>Let $k= \cap_{H \in S} \:H$
>1. Identity:
>   $1_{G} \in H \:\:\:\forall\:\:\:H \in S$
>   Thus
> 	  $1_{G} \in k$
>2. Inverses
>   Let $x \in H \:\:\:\forall\:\:\: H \in S$
>   As $H$ is a subgroup
>   $x^{-1} \in H \:\:\: \forall \:\:\: H \in S$
